Copenhagen Burnout Inventory

CBI - Kristensen, Borritz, Villadsen and Christensen, 2005

The Copenhagen Burnout Inventory measures burnout across three dimensions: personal, work-related, and client-related exhaustion. Unlike the MBI, it does not require a specific profession and is validated for any type of worker. In the creative industry, burnout often manifests differently in each dimension - a performer may score high on personal exhaustion but low on client-related burnout, while a manager may show the opposite pattern.

Burnout is not a personal failure.

Burnout is a systemic response to prolonged exposure to demands that exceed resources. It is common, it is treatable, and you do not need to push through it alone. Our team can help you understand your burnout profile and build a path toward recovery.

Important notice: The CBI is a validated research and clinical tool. Scores are presented as percentages where higher means more burnout. A score above 50 on any subscale warrants attention. This is not a clinical diagnosis.
