CAGE - Ewing, 1984. Validated in 50+ countries

The CAGE questionnaire is one of the most widely used and validated tools for detecting alcohol dependence in clinical settings. Its four questions cut through denial and social desirability bias by focusing on the emotional and behavioral consequences of drinking rather than quantity consumed. Particularly relevant for creative industry workers who use alcohol to manage performance anxiety, social fatigue, or irregular sleep patterns.

About this tool: CAGE is an acronym. Each letter represents one question.

C
Cut down - Have you felt you should cut down?
A
Annoyed - Have people annoyed you by criticizing?
G
Guilty - Have you felt guilty about drinking?
E
Eye-opener - Have you needed a drink to start the day?

Instructions: Please answer Yes or No to each question. Your answers are completely confidential.
